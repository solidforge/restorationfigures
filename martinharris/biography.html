Important People in the Restoration of the Gospel of Jesus Christ

Martin Harris

Martin Harris reportedly investigated Quakers, Universalists, Restorationists, Baptists, Methodists, and presbyterians. He is important in the history of the Church of Jesus Chris of Latter Day Saints because he assisted Joseph Smith as a scribe during the translation of the first portion of the Book of Mormon. He became one of the Three Witnesses of the Book of Mormon in June 1829, and was baptized into the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints by Oliver Cowdery on April 6, 1830. He paid for printing costs for the publication of the Book of Mormon through the sale of 151 acres. He led the saints from Palmyra to Kirtland in 1831, and was ordained a high priest there. He was appointed to serve a mission to Missouri on June 6, 1831. In later years, he was excommunicated from the church, but was rebaptized two times in his life.
